  FOR ALL CHARACTERS:

  1. No line longer than 3 sentences on screen at once.
     Break into multiple dialogue boxes if needed.
     Players read on phones. Respect the scroll.

  2. Every line should either ADVANCE THE PLOT or REVEAL CHARACTER.
     If it does neither, cut it.

  3. Memory-aware lines ALWAYS take priority over generic lines.
     If there's a relevant memory, USE IT. That's the whole point.

  4. Never explain the game mechanics through dialogue.
     Characters talk about the WORLD, not the SYSTEM.
     Carmen says "the market is cold" not "this gives a -10 penalty."

  5. Characters disagree with each other.
     Carmen thinks Ricky is dramatic. Ricky thinks Carmen is soulless.
     Arthur thinks Max overpromises. Max thinks Arthur is scared.
     These tensions are expressed through references, not arguments.

  6. Danny is the only character who breaks the fourth wall (slightly).
     His texts feel like they're written to the PLAYER, not the character.
     This is intentional. He's the emotional bridge.

  7. Nova is never cartoonish. She's never evil. She's never stupid.
     She's a real person with a different philosophy. Respect her.

  8. The Brother-in-Law is the secret heart of the game.
     If the player invests in him, the emotional payoff is enormous.
     If they ignore him, he's just a joke option.
     BOTH paths are valid. BOTH should feel complete.
